History

For many decades, humans are not aware that they contaminate sources of drinking water with raw sewage. So it had to diseases such as cholera and typhoid. For example, water pollution had intensified during the Industrial Era. This is a time where factories began releasing pollutants directly into waterways.

However before 2008, Auckland in New Zealand is the reigion that has the most polluted waterways. Even in recent years, Auckland is still considered to have the most polluted waterways, despite continous efforts from sustainable Coastlines to clean waterways. For example, 62 percent of rivers and lakes have been graded poor by the Ministry for the Environment for swimming, and 0 percent have been graded good.

Fortunately, an organisation known as Sustainable Coastlines have been hard at work cleaning our coastlines. More importantly, it all began back in 2008.

2008..

Back in 2008, Sam Judd had been travelling with Kiwi buddies; James Bailey and Zach Beard. They all had voluntered for the Galápagos National Park. Yet they were all shocked to find uninhabited coasts spoiled with litter, they had remvoved 1.6 tonnes within 8 days.

Despite a near-fatal tiger shark attack, Sam got right back to work with James and Zack. They all motivated 300 locals to remove 7.5 tonnes of litter from the remote San Cristóbal Island. Therefore, the trio pledged to continue their efforts to back in the Land of the Long White Cloud. However, the idea for Sustainable Coastlines had been born.

2009..

2009, the year when Sustainable Coastlines had been launched. They had launched their first signature event; a clean up of Great Barrier Island. Sam had joined forces with James, Camden Howard along with a team of organisers to bring together 700 volunteers from schools, the mainland, and the local community for a massive clean up. It lasted over 2 days. However, they managed to remove 2.8 tonnes of litter. As a result, the event garnered huge media attention.

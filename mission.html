The Purpose

There is a reason that this website exists. It is because, this is a school project to help spread awareness to students that we need to keep our coastlines clean. However, there is an organisation out there in New Zealand known as Sustainable Coastlines.

They are an organisation where they focus on cleaning coastlines. Therefore, building this website basically supports their cause.

So I am giving students two options. You either support Sustainable Coastlines if you want oceans clean, or get on with your daily lives.

Mission

In New Zealand, there is an organisation known as Sustainable Coastlines. This organisation is a registered New Zealand charity. More importantly, they are on a mission to keep coastlines and waterways safe from pollution.

Therefore, they deliver coastal clean-up events, public awareness campaigns, educational programmes, and riparian planting projects. In addition, they work with local community groups, interns, volunteers, and ambassadors. They work with those people to enable them, to deliver those activities into their own communities.

Goals

Sustainable Coastlines love coasts and oceans, they want them litter free. They clean up beaches and deliver education to prevent litter, especially single-use plastics.

Rivers in New Zealand need our aid. As a result, they restore waterways by planting trees. Secondly, enable communities to scale-up local solutions. Finally, educate people on the issues concerning freshwater.

Various people created the challenges we face, and it has become our responsibility to clean our coastlines. So they use behaviour-focused education, inspiring events and training to encourage people to take care of the places they love.

Programmes

In order to achive their vision, they provide three core programmes. They all directly invlove communities in positive outcomes for the waterways and coastlines they admire.

Beach clean ups have been important to Sustainable Coastlines, since beginning. Plastic pollution however, is not solved simply by collecting it where it ends up. As a result, this program will provide education into the equation, delivering positive change in the everyday lives of people.

Keeping waterways free of litter is the main aspect of healthy waters, yet is not the only solution. This program enables practical action - tree planting - and education on pollution in our inland lakes and rivers. The purpose is to ensure the health of our waterways before they flow the sea.

Final Words..

1. Conserve water, so excess runoff and wastewater will not flow into the ocean.

2. Reduce pollutants by disposing herbicides, pesticides, and cleaning products properly.

3. Reduce waste by cutting down what you throw away.

4. Shop wisely. You can do this by bringing a reusable bag and choose sustainable seafood.

5. Use fuel efficient vehicles, carpool, or ride a bike. That is how you reduce vehicle pollution.

6. Use less energy so it does not overset your thermostat.

7. Follow "catch and release" practices, keep more fish alive.

8. Anchor in sandy areas far from coral and sea grasses. Adhere to "no wake" zones.

9. Survival and healthy habitat go hand in hand. Must treat with care.

10. Volunteer for cleanups hosted by Sustainable Coastlines. You can also get involved in protecting your watershed as well.
